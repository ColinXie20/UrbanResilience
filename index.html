Welcome

This site explores existing sustainable design and proposes new designs that can be incorporated to ensure that cities can not just recover from natural disasters, but also thrive. We believe in creative solutions that stem from everyday life to live more sustainably. Through our interactive map, you can see our blueprint for a resilient city.

Green Urban Design Elements

Green Roofs

Roofs planted with local indigenous vegetation can reduce runoff and protect against flooding. They also encourage water infiltration and help to regulate surface temperatures, which saves money on cooling and heating.

Permeable Pavements

Using materials such as permeable concrete and permeable asphalt, pavements can slow down the flow of water and naturally infiltrate it to the groundwater. But we also need to take care of our city by cleaning these pavements before they are too clogged by debris!

Urban Trees

These trees not just beautify our cities can also provide necessary shades when heat waves hit. According to Yale Climate Connections, they prevent one premature death for every 100 trees planted!

Bike Lanes

Bike lanes are essential for encouraging a low carbon lifestyle as they not only ensure safety for the bikers but also mitigate almost exactly the same amount of GHG emissions per year created by highway development when comparing dollar-for-dollar spending.

Wildlife Crossings

Green bridges and tunnels can link fragmented habitats and ensure biodiversity and reduction in motor accidents, as fewer animals need to cross dangerous highways. These are essential for both human and animal safety as roadkill becomes a serious problem with more than a million animals killed every day.
